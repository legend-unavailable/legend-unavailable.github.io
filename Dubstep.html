Dubstep is usually what comes to mind when talking about any electronic music, however the "Dubstep" that most people know isn't really dubstep but we'll get to that. Dubstep or Classical Dubstep, for those that want the correct term, started in the UK durting the 90s and was at its peak in the 2000s. It is charcterized by a unqiue "deep" kick and a deep "wobbly" sub-bass. Now, modern Dubstep is not the same as Classical Dubstep. Why do I say that? The Dubstep that most people imagine is very different from Classical Dubstep, to the point that it's often considered a different sub-genre. This sub-genre is called Brostep and its differences are that it has a lot of influences from Metal and has a very agressive and artificial/robotic sounding bass. Other sub-genres include Drumstep, which is a combination of mainstream Dubstep and DnB which gives it a faster tempo, and Melodic Dubstep which, as the name implies, has a nice melodic bassline. Some of my personal favorite atists include Skrillex, RIOT, Bossfight, and MitiS.
